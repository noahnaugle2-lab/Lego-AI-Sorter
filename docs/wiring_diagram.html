Critical Safety Notes
Power sequencing: Always connect 12V stepper supply first, then 5V servo supply, then Raspberry Pi USB-C last
Common ground: All three power supplies MUST share a common ground reference
Current limits: Ensure wire gauges match specifications (18-20 AWG for low-current signals, 16 AWG for 5V servo rail)
Cable management: Keep signal lines away from high-current power rails to minimize EMI
GPIO voltage: Raspberry Pi GPIO operates at 3.3V; ensure pullup/pulldown resistors on inputs

Build & Verification Checklist
Before Power-On:
☐ All ground connections verified (3 PSUs share common GND)
☐ 5V servo rail properly sized (16 AWG minimum)
☐ 12V motor supply isolated from 5V rails
☐ I2C pullup resistors installed (4.7kΩ on SDA/SCL)
☐ TMC2209 microstep configuration verified
☐ MOSFET gate resistor installed (10kΩ recommended)
☐ All GPIO connections double-checked against pinout
Power-On Sequence:
1. Connect 12V stepper supply (no current should flow yet)
2. Connect 5V servo supply (PCA9685 boards power up)
3. Connect Raspberry Pi via USB-C last
4. Verify no unusual heat or smoke
Testing:
☐ I2C scan finds both PCA9685 boards (0x40 & 0x41)
☐ Servo test sweeps all 31 gates smoothly
☐ Stepper motor rotates in both directions
☐ LED strip PWM control responds to brightness commands
☐ IR sensor (if installed) detects bricks at exit
